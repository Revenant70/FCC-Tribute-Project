Marie Curie

The woman who saved the lives of over 1 million soldiers

  • November 7, 1867. Marie is born in Poland.
  • 1891. Marie goes to Paris to study at Sorbonne.
  • 1895. Marie marries Pierre Curie.
  • 1897. Marie's first daughter, Irene, is born.
  • 1902. Marie discoveres pure radium.
  • 1903. Marie and her husband win a Nobel Prize
  • 1904. Marie gets promoted to the chief assistant to her husband in his laboratory.
  • 1904. Pierre begins teaching at Sorbonne.
  • 1906. Marie's husband, Pierre, was killed in an accident. Marie takes his place teaching at Sorbonne and was the first woman professor to teach there.
  • 1911. Marie wins her second Nobel Prize, this time on her own, for her continued work in radioactivity.
  • 1914. During WWI, Marie uses her radioactive research materials to do x-rays on wounded soldiers to find bullets and shrapnel.
  • 1922. Marie becomes a member of the French Academy of Medicine. She devotes herself to using her knowledge to help people.
  • 1932. Marie and her sister start a research facility to help fight cancer.
  • 1934. Marie Curie dies
